📊 BUSINESS TRACK

L1 · Innovation & Entrepreneurship

Innovation = creating + implementing new ideas that generate value (economic/social/tech). Not just invention — must create value.

Entrepreneurship = spotting opportunities, mobilizing resources, creating value via new ventures. The 4th factor of production (land, labor, capital, + entrepreneurship).

Innovation Sweet Spot = overlap of 3 circles:

  • Desirability — people/users want it
  • Feasibility — technology can build it
  • Viability — business can sustain it
All 3 circles must overlap for good innovation.

L1 · Design Thinking Process

Stanford d.school 5 stages:

Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test

Starts with people & needs, not tech or business models. Airbnb: found bad host photos → sent photographers → solved signup problem.

DT vs Entrepreneurship: DT = what to build; Entrepreneurship = how to deliver & scale.

L1 · Mindset, Teams & Agility

3Ms: Mind (mindset/direction), Market (the map of value), Money (the fuel — manage wisely).

Growth Mindset: abilities grow with effort; failure = learning. Traits: Curiosity, Vision, Risk Tolerance, Resilience, Adaptability, Persistence.

Business Agility: adapt fast — rapid experimentation, continuous learning, iterative dev, flexible decisions.

High-Performance Teams: collaborate, not just talented. CATme = peer-feedback system → accountability + spots contribution gaps.

L2 · PEST Analysis

External macro-environment scan. Used when entering a new market.

PoliticalPolicies, regulation, stability, licenses, public funding
EconomicInflation, interest rates, employment, purchasing power, capital access
SocialCulture, demographics, education, lifestyle trends
TechnologicalInternet/mobile, infrastructure, emerging tech, digital tools
PEST ≠ PESTEL. PESTEL adds Environmental + Legal (Design track).

L2 · SWOT Analysis

StrengthsInternal + (what you do well)
WeaknessesInternal − (what you lack)
OpportunitiesExternal + (trends, gaps)
ThreatsExternal − (competition, regulation)

S+W = Internal; O+T = External.

PEST informs the O and T of SWOT.

L3 · Entrepreneurship Approaches

Lean Startup: Build-Measure-Learn loop. MVP = smallest build to test a hypothesis. Pivot or persevere on validated learning. Dropbox: demo video before coding.

Agile: iterative sprints, continuous delivery + feedback, flexible direction.

Tech-led vs Design-led: "built it because we could" vs "because they needed it." Best = both, but start with human need.

Social Entrepreneurship: social value via business methods. Grameen Bank microfinance.

Lean = eliminate waste/validate fast; Agile = adapt in cycles. They complement, not compete.

L4 · Business Model Canvas (9 blocks)

Right = market/customer · Center = value · Left = operations · Bottom = money. Asks: Desirable? Feasible? Viable?

Customer SegmentsWho you serve (demo/geo/behavior/psycho). No segment = no customer.
Value PropositionUnique value; ties to Jobs/Pains/Gains
ChannelsSales / communication / distribution
Customer Rel.Personal / Self-service / Automated
Revenue StreamsSales, fees, subscription, licensing, ads
Key ResourcesPhysical, intellectual, human, financial
Key ActivitiesProduction, marketing/sales, distribution, service
Key PartnersSuppliers, distributors, alliances, investors
Cost StructureFixed + variable; economies of scale/scope
Mistakes: too many segments (pick a beachhead!), vague VP, unrealistic revenue, ignoring costs.

L5 · Value Proposition Canvas

Customer Profile (right): Jobs (functional/emotional/social), Pains (frustrations/risks), Gains (desired outcomes).

Value Map (left): Products & Services, Pain Relievers, Gain Creators.

Fit = value map matches customer profile.

VP statement: "[Product] helps [customer] achieve [benefit] by [solving pain]." Uber: fast, reliable, affordable ride at a tap — no waiting/negotiation/cash.

L5 · Markets & B2B vs B2C

Market = buyers + sellers exchange. Types: consumer, financial, business, online.

B2BRational, long cycles, many deciders, ROI-driven
B2CEmotional, short cycles, personal benefit
If the user isn't the payer → system game (B2B dynamics).

L5 · Diffusion of Innovation

Innovators 2.5%Risk-takers, try first
Early Adopters 13.5%Visionaries, spot benefit early
Early Majority 34%Pragmatists, risk-averse
Late Majority 34%Skeptics, price-sensitive
Laggards 16%Traditionalists, last
SOM targets Innovators + Early Adopters.

L5 · TAM / SAM / SOM ★

TAM = total demand if 100% adopt (whole market).

SAM = portion you can serve (geo/constraints).
SAM = TAM × target segment %

SOM = portion captured short-term (1–3 yrs).
SOM = SAM × market share %

Uber Jordan: TAM ~2.4M, SAM ~920K (Amman+Irbid), SOM ~110–120K (12%).

SOM = your "entry wedge." SAM covers Innovators→Early Majority; SOM = Innovators+Early Adopters.

L6 · Competitor Analysis

Competitor types: Direct (same product+audience), Indirect (diff product, same need), Emerging (disruptors).

Benchmarking → compare vs rivals. Moon Chart = radar/spider chart for multi-attribute comparison.

Brand Positioning / Perceptual Map = plot brands on 2 attributes (e.g. Price vs Quality) → reveals market gaps.

USP = Unique + Valuable + Clear. = "what customers want" ∩ "what rivals do poorly."

Benchmarking shows where you outperform → that becomes your USP.

L6 · Pricing Strategies ★

Cost-PlusPrice = cost + markup %. Ignores willingness to pay.
Value-BasedPrice on perceived value; needs differentiation
CompetitiveMatch rivals (low differentiation)
PenetrationLow entry price → gain share fast → raise later
SkimmingHigh price first (early adopters) → lower over time. iPhone launch.
Psychological$9.99, charm/prestige, bundling, anchoring, scarcity

L7 · 4Ps Marketing Mix

ProductFeatures, quality, design, branding. Core vs augmented. Lifecycle: Intro→Growth→Maturity→Decline
PriceSignals value, positions brand (see L6)
PlaceDistribution: direct / indirect / digital. How + WHERE + WHEN accessed.
PromotionAdvertising, PR, social, content, sales promo. IMC = consistent message.

7Ps (services) adds: People, Process, Physical Evidence.

Each P must align with the others and the target customer.

L8 · Cost, Revenue & Finance

Finance helps: survive, plan, grow, reduce uncertainty. Most startups fail from financial mismanagement, not bad ideas.

Mistakes: underestimate costs, overestimate revenue, spend before validating, poor cash flow.

Startup CostsOne-time to launch (equipment, legal, site, licenses)
Fixed CostsDon't change w/ volume (rent, salaries, software)
Variable CostsScale w/ volume (materials, packaging, delivery)

Total Cost = Fixed + (Variable/unit × Units)

Projected Revenue asks: WHO buys? WHAT? At what PRICE? Use bottom-up, avoid "1% of market" fallacy.

L9 · Financial Statements & Ratios ★

Income (P&L)Revenue − Costs = Profit over a period
Balance SheetAssets = Liabilities + Equity (snapshot)
Cash FlowActual cash in/out (≠ profit!)

Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS

Net Profit = Gross Profit − OpEx/tax/interest

Breakeven Units = Fixed ÷ (Price − Variable/unit)

Contribution Margin = Price − Variable/unit

Profit Margin = Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

ROI = (Gain − Cost) ÷ Cost × 100

Current Ratio = Current Assets ÷ Current Liab.

Profitable ≠ cash-positive — timing of cash flows matters. Burn rate = how fast reserves are spent.
🎨 DESIGN THINKING TRACK

L1 · Why Startups Fail + HCD

Fail: build for selves not users; start with solution not problem; same-background teams → same blind spots.

Bad: Idea→Build→Hope. Good: People→Problem→Solution.

Human-Centered Design = start with people's frustrations, needs, behavior. "Users describe solutions; designers discover needs."

DT = "Observation without Judgment." Your expertise is also a filter that hides reality.

L1 · Cognitive Biases

Halo EffectOne great feature blinds you to flaws
Similarity (Like-Me)Assume users behave like you
ConfirmationSeek info that supports your idea
StereotypingOver-generalize user groups

L1 · Human Hacks & Journey

Human Hack = non-standard workaround because a system failed a need. Where there's a workaround, there's an unmet need. (infra/design/trust/process failure)

Bootcamp journey:
Understand→Research→Define→Ideate→Prototype→Test→Evaluate→Pitch

"Business = destination. Design = the maze. Don't jump over it — walk through."

Decode complaints: "slides earlier" → control; "hate group projects" → fair workload; "bigger battery" → anxiety reduction. Always go one level deeper.

L2 · Observe · Engage · Immerse

Self-reporting fails: people don't do what they say / think / are told.

ObserveWatch in context — habits, workarounds, friction, hesitation. Don't intervene.
EngageAsk behavioral Qs (not opinions). Decode: checks phone=boredom, avoids speaking=discomfort.
ImmerseExperience it yourself → how it FEELS. Write with non-dominant hand.
Observed behavior > stated preference. Always.

L2 · Research Types

Primary (direct): observe, behavioral interviews, immersion.

Secondary (indirect): reports, stats, articles, existing data. Replaces assumptions w/ evidence.

Every insight = User + Need + Context.

"You think you understand a problem — you saw a symptom."

L3 · PESTEL + System Thinking

Business sees the MAP; Design sees the TRAP (what blocks people).

P E S T(as PEST) policies, economy, social, tech
EnvironmentalSustainability, climate, regulations
LegalEmployment, consumer protection, IP laws

System Thinking: user lives in a network. You sell to their father/friends/boss/system too. If user says YES but environment says NO → idea is dead.

L3 · Jobs-to-be-Done + Kano

JTBD — people "hire" products for a job:

  • Functional — the practical task
  • Social — how they appear to others
  • Emotional — how they feel

Not a drill — a hole to hang a photo to feel like a good parent.

Kano (value layers): Required (must-have) · Expected (baseline) · Desired (nice) · Unexpected (delight). Delights drift → become required over time.

L3 · Problem Statement Formula ★

[User] struggles with [situation] because [root cause], leading to [consequence].

Good: "Busy students skip meals at peak hours because food queues are unpredictable, leading to fatigue + lower performance."

Bad: "Students need better campus food" → vague, no root cause.

L4 · Personas & Beachhead

Myth of "everyone": if customer = everyone, you have no customer. "Students/People/Users" = categories, not segments.

Real user = specific situation + specific action + real struggle. "If you can't see the moment, you don't understand the problem."

Persona parts: When (situation) · What they do (behavior) · Avoid (pains) · Feel (emotional job).

Beachhead persona = Segment + real human + real moment + job + intensity.

Shark bite (urgent, act NOW) vs Mosquito bite (annoying, ignored). Beachhead needs a SHARK-bite problem.

L4 · Root Cause & Jargon

5 Whys → drill to root cause. Move "What is" → "What if." Defined the cage (problem)? Now find the exit (solution).

Jargon Assassin: jargon = symptom of unclear thinking. Explain to a 10-yr-old / grandmother in Amman. "Speak Human, Not Corporate."

L5 · How Might We (HMW) — 4 Types ★

HMW = decision about WHICH part of problem space to explore. Template: "HMW help [user] achieve [job] despite [pain]?"

1 Efficiency"reduce waiting time" → faster/optimize
2 Experience"reduce stress while waiting" → emotional
3 Behavior"change WHEN students eat" → new habits
4 System"redesign food distribution" → structural
The HMW type you choose determines the kind of solutions you'll get.

L5 · Divergence & Ideation

First idea = obvious. Generate MANY fundamentally different ideas. Rules: quantity over quality, no judging, different directions.

Wrong (same thinking): app w/ notifications, app w/ tracking, app w/ AI.

Right (diff thinking): eliminate queues, shift eating times, remove need for cafeteria.

Without HMW: different but irrelevant. With HMW: different AND relevant.

Communication: fillers (umm, like) = panic. The Pause = confidence. Slow down at "the because" (the insight).

L6 · Validation Engine + Double Diamond

Insight→Hypothesis→Prototype→Test→Learn→Decide

Double Diamond: Discover (expand) → Define (focus) → Develop (expand) → Deliver (focus). Define = hardest step (constructing the right problem). Develop = HMW zone.

Fatal mistake: testing HOW (usability) before WHY (value). Confirm they want it first.

L6 · Prototype Types

StoryboardTests journey/logic (6–8 steps + emotion). Can't test demand/interaction.
Role-PlayTests service/conversation. Depends on acting.
Interface (paper/digital)Tests screens/flow — watch wrong taps, hesitation.
Digital MVPReal interaction, 1 screen/flow/function. Risk: overbuilding.
Landing/Fake DoorTests DEMAND — "sign up" → watch clicks. Tests intent, not behavior.

Outdoor signals: "I would use this"→ignore (cheap talk); "Interesting but…"→dig; "When can I get it?"→real demand. Patterns > opinions.

L7 · Feedback Extraction

Feedback = extraction of behavior, friction, expectation gaps, commitment. You tested assumptions, not ideas. Testing w/o change = performance.

Evidence Chain: Observation → Insight → Decision → Alignment. Skip one = guessing.

"If you can't say this, you didn't learn": We observed ___ · This means ___ · So we changed ___ · This aligns with the user because ___.

L7 · Feedback Methods + RAT

Silent ObservationNo help/guidance — watch hesitation, breakdown
Thinking AloudUser narrates → where mental model breaks
Wizard of OzSimulate value manually w/o building tech
Commitment TestPush for action/effort. Refusal invalidates prior praise.

RAT = Riskiest Assumption Test: "if THIS is wrong → everything collapses." Define success rate AND failure threshold before testing. Test only with your persona. Never ask "Do you like it?"

L7 · Iterate / Pivot / Kill ★

Usability Friction (HOW)Wants value but blocked → Iterate (fix flow)
Value Deficit (WHY)Understands but doesn't care → Pivot (weak problem)
Mental Model MismatchUses it differently → Investigate (new opportunity)

Decision Layer: Iterate = real problem, weak solution · Pivot = wrong problem · Kill = no behavioral intent after multiple tests.

Cost of being wrong: MVP (fast, controlled) → Pilot (slow, limited) → Open Beta (can't hide) → Polished Launch (danger). "Launch when you can't iterate = gambling."

⚡ EXAM QUICK-REFERENCE & COMMON TRAPS

📐 Formula Bank (all math in one place)

Total Cost = Fixed + (Variable/unit × Units)

Contribution Margin (CM) = Price − Variable/unit

Breakeven Units = Fixed Costs ÷ CM

Breakeven Revenue = Breakeven Units × Price

Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS

Net Profit = Gross Profit − OpEx − tax − interest

Gross Profit Margin = Gross Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

Net Profit Margin = Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

ROI = (Gain − Cost) ÷ Cost × 100 = Net Profit ÷ Invested × 100

Current Ratio = Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities

SAM = TAM × target segment %

SOM = SAM × market share (conversion) %

🧮 Worked Example — Breakeven

Café: Fixed = 40,000 · Price = 4 · Variable = 1.50 · Sold = 12,000 cups.

  • CM = 4 − 1.50 = 2.50/cup
  • Total contribution = 12,000 × 2.50 = 30,000
  • Result = 30,000 − 40,000 = −10,000 (loss)
  • Breakeven = 40,000 ÷ 2.50 = 16,000 cups
Compare total contribution to fixed costs — don't compare revenue to fixed costs.

🧮 Worked Example — TAM/SAM/SOM

3M smartphone users · 60% health-conscious · 25% willing to pay · 10% Year-1 conversion.

  • TAM = 3,000,000 (all users)
  • SAM = 1,800,000 × 25% = 450,000
  • SOM = 450,000 × 10% = 45,000
Raise price → fewer "willing to pay" → SAM shrinks → SOM shrinks. Annual revenue = users × monthly price × 12.

⚠️ Most-Confused Distinctions

PEST vs PESTELPESTEL = PEST + Environmental + Legal
Fixed vs VariableFixed = same regardless of volume; Variable = scales w/ units
CapEx vs OpExCapEx = long-term asset (oven); OpEx = day-to-day running cost
Penetration vs SkimmingLow price to grab share vs High price first then lower
Pain vs GainPain = avoid (frustration); Gain = desired positive outcome
Strength vs OpportunityStrength = internal +; Opportunity = external +
Weakness vs ThreatWeakness = internal −; Threat = external −
Profit vs Cash FlowCan be profitable but cash-broke (timing of payments)

⚠️ Margin Reclassification Traps

  • Move cost COGS → OpEx: Gross Profit ↑ (GPM ↑), Net Profit unchanged (NPM same).
  • Move cost OpEx → COGS: Gross Profit ↓ (GPM ↓), NPM same.
  • Add depreciation to OpEx: NPM ↓, GPM unchanged.
  • Same Net Profit on lower revenue = higher NPM.
Moving a cost between lines never changes Net Profit — only where the cut appears.

🔑 High-Yield One-Liners

  • Innovation must create value, not just be invented.
  • No defined segment = no customer.
  • Observed behavior > stated preference.
  • Test WHY (value) before HOW (usability).
  • "I would use this" = cheap talk; "When can I get it?" = real demand.
  • Beachhead needs a shark-bite (urgent) problem.
  • Define = hardest step (construct the right problem).
  • If user says YES but their system says NO → idea dies.
