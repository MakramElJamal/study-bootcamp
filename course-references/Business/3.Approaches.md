WHAT IS A MVP?

-A Minimum Viable Product (MVP) refers to the simplest version of a new product that can be launched to the market.

-An MVP includes only the essential features needed to make the product functional and provide value to early users.

-The main goal of an MVP is to test a business idea quickly and gather feedback from real customers, allowing for iterative improvements and minimizing the risk of developing a product that does not meet market needs.

The key components of the Lean Startup approach are:

Build: Create a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that delivers the core value proposition to early adopters. This initial version should be simple and cost-effective to produce.

Measure: Collect data and feedback from users to see how the MVP is performing. Metrics should focus on key indicators that help validate or refute the product idea.

Learn: Use the feedback to make informed decisions. If the idea is validated, entrepreneurs can move forward and scale. If not, they might pivot and change direction based on new insights.

APPLYING THE METHODOLOGY

1.Identify a Problem: Start with a problem or need that your product aims to solve.

2.Create a Hypothesis: Form a hypothesis about how your product will solve that problem and who your target customers are.

3.Build an MVP: Develop the simplest version of your product to test your hypothesis.

4.Test and Measure: Launch the MVP and gather feedback from real users. Use metrics to measure its success.

5.Learn and Decide: Analyze the results to determine if you should pivot or persevere. Use the insights to improve your product or change your strategy.
