WHAT IS A MARKET

A market is a system or place where buyers and sellers interact to exchange goods, services, or information.

Key Characteristics:

1.Participants: Buyers and sellers.

2.Medium of Exchange: Currency, barter, or other trade mechanisms.

3.Scope: Can be local, regional, national, or global.

4.Type of Goods or Services: Tangible (products) or intangible (services, intellectual property).

STEPS MEASURING MARKET SIZE -TERMS

TAM (Total Addressable Market): The maximum demand if everyone who could possibly use the product adopted it.

SAM (Serviceable Available Market): The portion of TAM that your business can realistically target, given geography, regulation, or business model.

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): The portion of SAM you can realistically capture in the short term, based on your resources, competition, and entry strategy.

STEP1. DEFINE YOUR TARGET MARKET

What product?

Which geography?

Which customer segments? (demographic, behavior, industry)

STEP2 DETERMINE "TAM"

TAM is the total demand for a product or service in a particular market.

The "big picture" or the overall market size

The maximum revenue a business can generate if it captures 100% of the market

"How big is the entire market for this product?"

STEP3 CALCULATE "SAM"

The subset of TAM that aligns with your business's offerings and constraints, such as geographic location, product features, or operational focus

Focuses on the segment of the market that your product or service can realistically serve

Narrow TAM by Geography (licensing, logistics, practical limitations ).

Refine by Target Customer Segment (demographics, behavior, needs).

SAM = TAM × Target Market Segmentation

STEP4 ESTIMATE "SOM"

SOM is the portion of SAM that your business can realistically capture in the short term, typically within the first few years of operation (your initial target market).

Estimate Market Share based on competition, your capabilities, marketing, and distribution channels

Time Frame: Define the period for which you're calculating SOM (e.g., one year, three years).

SOM is typically a short-term projection based on your current capabilities.

SOM = SAM × Estimated Market Share (%)

TAM -SAM -SOM & CUSTOMER TYPES

Innovators + Early Adopters = SOM → the "entry wedge" you can realistically win early.

Early Majority (part of SAM) → your growth market once you cross the chasm.

Late Majority + Laggards (remaining TAM) → you only get to them once you are established.
